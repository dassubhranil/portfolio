Generative AI for Pathology Datasets

Featured - Research Project | Medical Imaging | GANs

Repo Case Study

Situation

[cite_start]Access to real patient data for medical imaging research is severely limited by HIPAA regulations[cite: 46]. This data scarcity made it difficult to develop effective nuclear detection models, which are crucial for the detection of cancerous cells.

Task

[cite_start]The objective was to leverage Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) to synthesize nuclear detection datasets[cite: 46]. [cite_start]This approach aimed to overcome the data access challenges imposed by HIPAA and provide high-quality synthetic data to facilitate the training of accurate nuclei detection models[cite: 46].

Action

[cite_start]I led a project to develop generative modeling pipelines that utilized advanced GAN architectures such as DCGAN, Variational Autoencoders, and StyleGAN3 to create synthetic pathology datasets[cite: 47]. [cite_start]These pipelines produced synthetic data that addressed the data scarcity challenge by retaining 95% of the critical biological signal from real samples[cite: 11]. [cite_start]The synthetic datasets were then used to train a YOLOv8 architecture to enhance the development of a nuclei detection model[cite: 47]. This comprehensive approach involved a multi-stage process from data synthesis to model training and validation.

Result

[cite_start]The project successfully enhanced the model's performance by 40%, with the YOLOv8 architecture achieving an impressive accuracy of 85% in nuclei detection[cite: 47]. [cite_start]By utilizing synthetic data, the research successfully addressed privacy concerns while contributing to the development of a more effective tool for cancer detection[cite: 46].
